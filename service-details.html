Seed Saving Workshops

Master the critical skills required to harvest, process, and store your own living seeds.

Overview

Hands-On Educational Experience

Our Seed Saving Workshops are designed for both novice gardeners and experienced farmers. Led by our master agronomists, these intensive sessions dive deep into the biological mechanics of plant reproduction and practical, hands-on saving techniques.

You'll learn not just the 'how', but the 'why'—understanding the crucial role seed saving plays in preserving localized biodiversity and ensuring future food security.

  • Comprehensive 2-day curriculum
  • Take home an extensive starter kit
  • Access to our exclusive grower's network

Why Take This Workshop?

Financial Independence

Never buy seeds again. Create a self-sustaining cycle in your garden that saves hundreds of dollars annually.

Local Adaptation

Seeds saved year after year adapt specifically to your microclimate and local soil conditions.

Community Sharing

Become a vital resource for your community by sharing resilient, locally adapted seed stock.

Curriculum

What You Will Learn

1

Botany Basics & Isolation

Understanding self-pollinators vs. cross-pollinators. Techniques for caging, bagging, and distance isolation to ensure pure genetics.

2

Harvesting Protocols

Identifying the exact moment a seed is mature and ready for harvest, which often differs wildly from eating maturity.

3

Processing: Wet vs. Dry

Hands-on practice with fermentation techniques for tomatoes and cucumbers, versus dry threshing for beans and grains.
